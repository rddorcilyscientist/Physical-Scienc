This page helps you explore how changing mass, speed, and height affects:

  • Kinetic Energy (KE) = energy of motion
  • Potential Energy (PE) = energy of position or height
  • Mechanical Energy (ME) = KE + PE

Play with the simulator, watch the graphs change, and answer the questions to check your understanding.

üìù Practice Questions ‚Äì Use the Simulator to Help You

  1. Set mass = 2 kg and speed = 3 m/s, height = 0 m.
    a) What is the KE?   b) What is the PE?   c) What is the ME?
  2. Keep mass = 2 kg and double the speed. By what factor did KE change? Explain using the formula KE = ¬Ω m v¬≤.
  3. Keep speed = 3 m/s and double the mass. Compare the new KE to the old KE. Is the relationship between KE and mass linear or quadratic?
  4. Set height = 1 m, then triple the height. How does PE change? Is the relationship between PE and height linear or quadratic?
  5. Choose your own values for m, v, and h. Record KE, PE, and ME. Then change only one parameter (mass, speed, or height) and describe what happened.
